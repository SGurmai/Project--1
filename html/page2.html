13th Century

In the 13th century, armor underwent significant developments and changes, reflecting the evolving nature of warfare and advancements in metallurgy and blacksmithing techniques. Here's an overview of armor during the 13th century:

Helmets

In the 13th century, helmets varied depending on the region, cultural influence, and the technological advancements of the time. Here are some types of helmets that were used during this period:

Nasal Helmet:

Also known as a nasal helm or nose guard helm, this helmet featured a nasal bar extending down over the nose to provide additional protection to the face. It was commonly worn by infantry and cavalry during the 13th century.

Spangenhelm:

Spangenhelms were constructed from metal plates riveted together, often with reinforcing bands (spangen) along the helmet's circumference. They typically had a conical or rounded skull and often featured a nasal guard for facial protection.

Kettle Hat (or Chapel de Fer):

This helmet had a brim that extended outward to protect the wearer's face from blows and projectiles. It was commonly worn by infantry and was popular throughout the medieval period, including the 13th century.

Great Helm (or Pot Helm):

This was a popular type of helmet in the 13th century, especially among knights. It was a large, fully enclosing helmet with a flat or conical top and a T-shaped opening for the eyes and mouth. Great helms provided excellent protection but restricted vision and ventilation.
