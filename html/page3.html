14th Century

The 14th century marked a continued evolution in armor technology and usage, influenced by the changing dynamics of warfare and advancements in metallurgy. This period saw refinements in existing armor types as well as the emergence of new defensive gear to meet the challenges of combat. At the outset of the century, plate armor began to gain prominence, gradually supplanting chain mail as the primary form of protection for knights and elite warriors. Innovations in forging techniques allowed for the creation of larger, articulated plates that offered superior coverage without sacrificing mobility. Furthermore, the 14th century witnessed the widespread adoption of firearms, particularly in the latter half of the century. This necessitated modifications to existing armor designs to withstand the ballistic impact of bullets and projectiles, leading to the development of specialized plate configurations and reinforced helmets. In addition to plate armor, padded garments such as gambesons continued to be essential components of a warrior's defensive ensemble. These quilted layers provided crucial protection against blunt force trauma and served as an effective supplement to metal armor. The 14th century also saw advancements in helmet design, with various styles catering to different combat scenarios and preferences. From the iconic great helm to more specialized visored helmets, head protection evolved to offer a balance of defense, visibility, and comfort. In summary, the 14th century witnessed a period of innovation and adaptation in the realm of armor, driven by the demands of e volving warfare and the relentless pursuit of improved protection on the battlefield.

Helmets

In the 14th century, helmets continued to be vital pieces of protective gear for warriors across different regions and cultures. Here are some notable helmet types that were prevalent during this period:

Bascinet:

The bascinet was a popular helmet choice in the 14th century. It featured a close-fitting skullcap with an attached visor or aventail to protect the face and neck. Bascinets offered improved visibility and ventilation compared to earlier helmet designs.

Sallet:

The sallet emerged as a versatile helmet option during the 14th century. Characterized by its elongated skull and distinct visor, the sallet provided excellent protection to the head while allowing for enhanced mobility and visibility on the battlefield.

Visored Helmet:

Visored helmets became increasingly popular in the 14th century, offering enhanced protection to the face while allowing for adjustable visibility. These helmets featured a hinged visor that could be raised or lowered as needed, providing flexibility in combat situations.

These are just a few examples of the diverse range of helmets that were used by warriors during the 14th century, reflecting the ongoing evolution of defensive technology and the varied needs of combatants.

Chest armor

Throughout the 14th century, chest armor remained a critical component of a warrior's defensive gear, offering vital protection to the torso and vital organs in the midst of battle. Here are some of the prominent chest armor types from this period:

Plate Cuirass:

The plate cuirass became increasingly prevalent in the 14th century, replacing chain mail as the primary form of torso protection for knights and elite warriors. Consisting of articulated metal plates, the cuirass offered superior defense against both slashing and piercing attacks, while still allowing for a reasonable range of motion.

Mail Hauberk:

Despite the rise of plate armor, chain mail hauberks remained in use during the 14th century, particularly among infantry and less affluent warriors. These flexible mesh shirts provided reliable protection against slashing attacks and were often worn in conjunction with padded garments for added defense.

Brigandine:

The brigandine gained popularity as a versatile form of torso protection in the 14th century. Consisting of small metal plates riveted to a fabric or leather base, the brigandine offered a balance of flexibility and defense, making it a favored choice among soldiers and mercenaries.

These are just a few examples of the diverse range of chest armor that was utilized during the 14th century. The choice of armor often depended on factors such as the wearer's social status, combat role, and regional preferences, highlighting the adaptability and evolution of defensive technologies during this period.
